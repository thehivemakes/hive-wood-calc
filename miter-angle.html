How to Calculate Miter Angles

A miter joint connects two pieces at an angle. For a regular polygon (all sides equal), the miter angle is simply 180 divided by the number of sides. A square frame has 4 sides, so the miter angle is 180 ÷ 4 = 45 degrees.

For non-square corners (like a bay window or custom frame), you can enter the corner angle directly. The miter angle is half the supplement of the corner angle: miter = (180 − corner angle) ÷ 2.

On most miter saws, the angle scale reads from 0° (straight cut) to 45° or beyond. The "miter angle" result from this calculator is the angle to set on your saw fence.
