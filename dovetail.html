How to Space Dovetails Evenly

Dovetail joints are one of the strongest and most visually striking woodworking joints. Even spacing creates a professional look and ensures the joint's mechanical strength is distributed evenly across the board.

The layout starts with two half-pins at each edge of the board. The remaining space is divided between tails and full pins. The tail-to-pin ratio determines the visual character: a 2:1 ratio is traditional (tails are twice as wide as pins), while 3:1 creates a more delicate look for fine furniture.

This calculator determines the exact width of each tail and pin given your board width, number of tails, half-pin width, and desired ratio. Mark these measurements directly on your board for precise hand-cutting.
